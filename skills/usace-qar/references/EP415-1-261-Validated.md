# OFFICIAL USACE QAR GUIDANCE - EP 415-1-261 Series

## The Three Phase Control Process (Validated from EP 415-1-261 Vol 6)

### Phase 1: Preparatory
- Review contract documents, specs, drawings
- Verify submittals comply with requirements
- Ensure contractor understands scope
- Verify materials available and approved
- Review schedule and sequence

### Phase 2: Initial
- First representative sample of work
- All critical inspections performed
- Establish correct standard of workmanship
- Identify and correct deficiencies before continuing

### Phase 3: Follow-up
- Ongoing verification
- Work proceeds as established in Initial phase
- Continuous monitoring throughout installation

---

## Key QAR Responsibilities (from EP 415-1-261)

1. **Thorough Review of Contract Documents** - Latest drawings/specs available and understood
2. **Submittal Review** - Verify compliance with contract, Buy American, manufacturer instructions
3. **Shop Drawing Verification** - Proper interface of envelope components
4. **Schedule and Sequence** - Proper sequence to allow correct installation
5. **Mock-up Oversight** - Accurate representation of specified assemblies
6. **Testing Coordination** - Required testing identified, scheduled, competent testers
7. **Discrepancy Management** - All changes flow through RFI/DOR channels
8. **Material Inspection** - Visual inspection BEFORE incorporation
9. **Workmanship Assessment** - Meets contract AND manufacturer requirements

## Building Envelope Critical Points (Vol 6)

### Control Layers
- **Air Control** - Continuous air barrier
- **Vapor Control** - Proper placement per climate
- **Water Control** - Water-resistant layer, drainage plane

### Interface Points (Critical)
- Wall to roof
- Wall to foundation/slab
- Window/door frames to wall
- Penetrations (pipes, vents, conduits)

### Materials to Inspect
- Exterior cladding - moisture typology, water-resistant layer
- Masonry - mortar joints, control joints, weep holes
- Fenestration - installation, seals, flashing
- Roofing - underlayment, membranes, flashings
- Insulation - complete coverage, no thermal bridges
- Sealants - continuity at all transitions
