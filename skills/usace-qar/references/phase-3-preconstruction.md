# Phase 3: Pre-Construction (Pre-Con)

Pre-Con is the phase after award, before construction starts. The QAR receives project documents, attends kickoff, and prepares for inspections.

## What Happens in Pre-Con

### 1. Receive Project Documents
- Contract (SOW, terms, conditions)
- Specifications (full spec book)
- Drawings (approved-for-construction set)
- Schedule (baseline)
- Contractor submittal register

### 2. Kickoff Meeting
- Introductions (COR, Contractor, Subs, PM)
- Review scope and schedule
- Establish communication channels
- Define reporting requirements

### 3. Document Review
- QAR reviews full specs and drawings
- Identifies critical inspection points
- Notes areas requiring special attention
- Compares specs vs. drawings (Design Review complete here)

### 4. Prepare for Construction
- Establish inspection procedures
- Set up daily report format
- Identify access requirements
- Coordinate with contractor on work hours, safety

---

## QAR Deliverables in Pre-Con

- [ ] Confirm receipt of all specs/drawings
- [ ] Complete Design Review (if not done)
- [ ] Review contractor's QC plan
- [ ] Confirm submittal schedule
- [ ] Verify contractor insurance/bonds
- [ ] Establish deficiency reporting format
- [ ] Set up daily report log

---

## Common Pre-Con Issues

1. **Missing documents** — Not all specs/drawings received
2. **Outdated specs** — Using previous revision
3. **Schedule conflicts** — Unrealistic dates
4. **Submittal gaps** — Critical items missing from register
5. **QC plan incomplete** — Contractor hasn't defined inspection procedures

---

## Transition to Construction

Once Pre-Con is complete and contractor has:
- Approved submittals for critical items
- Scheduled the work
- QA/QC procedures in place

...construction can begin. QAR moves to Inspection phase.
