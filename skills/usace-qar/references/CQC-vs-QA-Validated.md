# Contractor Quality Control vs Government Quality Assurance (EP 715-1-2)

## Official USACE Guidance

From EP 715-1-2 "A Guide to Effective Contractor Quality Control (CQC)"

---

## Three Phases of Control (Contractor CQC)

### 1. Preparatory Phase (In Advance of Construction)
- Review/approve shop drawings, lift sheets, test reports, mix designs
- Physical check of materials on site against approvals
- Safety checks of equipment
- Advance planning

### 2. Initial Phase (At Start of Each Feature of Work)
- Get work started properly in compliance with contract
- Establish standards of workmanship
- Discuss/settle differences in interpretation
- Perform preventive inspection

### 3. Follow-up Phase (Continuous)
- Ongoing inspection/testing
- Determine continuation of compliance
- Daily or routine basis

---

## Contractor Responsibilities (CQC)

- Produce quality product on time, in compliance with contract
- Establish and utilize CQC program
- Maintain records of QC activities
- Develop CQC plan
- Provide adequate CQC staff
- Outline procedures for submittals
- Preventive control for each DFOW
- Testing coverage
- Documentation (reports showing compliance)

---

## Government Responsibilities (QA/QAR)

- Establish construction standards
- Check adequacy of contractor's control
- Perform specified tests and inspections
- Determine deficiencies corrected
- Determine payments due
- Assure timely completion

---

## Key Distinction

| Contractor (CQC) | Government (QAR) |
|------------------|------------------|
| **Manages and controls** quality | **Assures** contractor is managing quality |
| Day-to-day execution | Ultimate acceptance |
| Prevents deficiencies | Verifies prevention worked |
| Documents their QC | Verifies contractor's documentation |

---

## QAR's Core Role

The QAR verifies the **adequacy and effectiveness** of the contractor's control system - not just individual deficiencies. When deficiencies are found, QAR emphasizes inadequacies in the control system itself.

**This is the check-and-balance system:**

1. Contractor inspects their own work (CQC)
2. QAR verifies contractor inspected (QA)
3. QAR catches what QC missed

---

*EP 715-1-2 validates our understanding of the CQC/QA relationship.*
