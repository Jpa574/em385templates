# UFGS 21 13 00.00 40 - Fire-Suppression Sprinkler Systems

## Key Spec Highlights for QAR

### Materials
- **Underground Piping:** Cast iron (AWWA C151) or Ductile iron (AWWA C151), Class 150, 1500mm cover minimum
- **Aboveground (Wet):** Black Carbon Steel (BCS) - Schedule 40 per ASTM A53
- **Aboveground (Dry/Galvanized):** Galvanized Carbon Steel (GCS) - ASTM A53
- **Fittings:** Cast iron screwed (ASTM A126) or grooved couplings
- **Sprinkler Heads:** Per design - check temperature ratings

### Critical Inspection Points
1. **Underground**
   - Pipe coating/lining (bituminous or mortar)
   - Joint type (mechanical vs push-on)
   - Thrust blocks installed

2. **Aboveground**
   - Hanger/support spacing per MSS SP-58
   - Pipe sizing matches design
   - Grooved fittings properly made up

3. **Testing**
   - Hydrostatic test: 200 psi or 1.5x working pressure (whichever higher) for 2 hours
   - Air test for dry systems
   - Flow test at design density

4. **Valves & Alarms**
   - Alarm valve (wet or dry) UL/FM approved
   - Waterflow alarm device installed
   - Fire department connections correct thread type (NFPA 1963)

### Documentation Required
- Shop Drawings
- Product Data (sprinklers, valves, pipe/fitting specs)
- Test Reports (hydrostatic, flow)
- Manufacturer certifications

### Common Deficiencies
- Missing thrust blocks at bends/tees
- Improper hanger spacing (>10' for wet, >8' for dry)
- Incorrect sprinkler temperature rating for location
- No drip legs at low points
- Alarm valve not properly configured
