# PART 1: SAFETY (Deep Knowledge)

## QAR Role in Safety

**QAR does NOT own safety** — that's the Contractor's duty. But QAR has responsibilities:
- Stop work for **imminent danger** (life-threatening)
- Report unsafe conditions
- Document concerns in daily reports
- **Never direct** contractor on safety corrections — that's their competent person

## OSHA 1926 - Key Areas QAR Must Know

### 1. Fall Protection (1926.500)
- **Requirement:** Guardrails, safety nets, or personal fall arrest systems
- **Floor openings:** Covered or guarded
- **Roof work:** Fall protection required at 6' (construction)
- **QAR action:** Stop work immediately if fall protection missing over 6'

### 2. Scaffolding (1926.451)
- **Capacity:** Designed to support 4x intended load
- **Guardrails:** Required at 10' (middle rail + toe board)
- **Access:** Ladder or stairway within 4x height
- **Base:** Foundation sound, wheels locked
- **QAR action:** Verify scaffold tags, check guardrails before allowing use

### 3. Excavations (1926.651)
- **Classification:** Soil must be classified by competent person
- **Protective systems:** Sloping, shoring, or shield
- **Access:** Within 25' of workers
- **Utilities:** Utilities located before digging
- **QAR action:** Verify soil classification, check for protective system

### 4. Electrical (1926.403-.408)
- **GFCI:** Required for all temporary power
- **Equipment:** Ground fault protection
- **Overhead lines:** Clearance required
- **QAR action:** Check GFCI on all temp power, look for exposed wiring

### 5. Cranes & Rigging (1926.550)
- **Operator:** Certified/qualified
- **Load chart:** Available and followed
- **Signals:** Qualified signal person
- **Ground conditions:** Firm, level
- **QAR action:** Verify operator certs, check load charts

### 6. Fire Protection (1926.150)
- **Fire extinguishers:** 10lb ABC minimum, 150' travel max
- **Flammable storage:** Proper cabinets
- **Fire watch:** When welding/cutting
- **QAR action:** Check extinguishers, verify fire watch for hot work

### 7. PPE (1926.95)
- **Required:** Hard hats, safety glasses, safety shoes, high-vis
- **Contractor:** Must provide PPE
- **QAR action:** Verify workers have required PPE

### 8. Hazard Communication (1926.59)
- **SDS:** Must be on site for all chemicals
- **Labels:** All containers labeled
- **Training:** Workers trained on hazards
- **QAR action:** Check SDS availability, verify labeling

### 9. Confined Spaces (1926.1201)
- **Permit-required:** Ventilation, atmospheric testing, rescue plan
- **Entry team:** Attendant, entry supervisor
- **QAR action:** Know location of confined spaces, verify permits

### 10. Trenching & Shoring (1926.652)
- **Depth >5':** Protective system required
- **Spoil pile:** 2' from edge minimum
- **Access:** Within 25' of workers
- **QAR action:** Check daily before allowing trench entry

## USACE Safety Requirements

### EM 385-1-1 (USACE Safety Manual)
- **More stringent** than OSHA in many areas
- **Safety and Health Requirements Manual**
- Applies to all USACE projects

### Key USACE Additions:
- **Weekly safety meetings** documented
- **Monthly safety inspections** by contractor
- **Daily tailgate safety talks**
- **QAR documentation** of all safety concerns

---

## Stop Work Authority

**QAR MUST stop work when:**
1. Imminent danger to life or limb
2. Unsafe condition not being addressed
3. Worker without required PPE
4. Unprotected fall hazard
5. Unstable excavation
6. Unsafe scaffolding
7. Unsafe electrical

**Procedure:**
1. Stop work verbally
2. Notify contractor in writing (daily report)
3. Notify COR/PM immediately
4. Document in daily report

## Common Safety Deficiencies QAR Catches

| Hazard | What to Look For |
|--------|-----------------|
| Falls | No guardrails, uncovered floor openings |
| Struck-by | Unsecured materials, overhead loads |
| Caught-in | Unprotected trench, pinch points |
| Electrical | Damaged cords, no GFCI |
| PPE | No hard hats, no safety glasses |
| Scaffolding | Missing guardrails, unstable base |
| Fire | No extinguisher for hot work |
| Housekeeping | Trip hazards, clutter |

# PART 2: CORE CAPABILITIES

## Quick Start

**Need spec info?** Ask: "What's required for fire suppression pipe testing?" or "What conduit type is spec'd for wet locations?"

**Got a submittal?** Upload or paste the product data and ask: "Does this meet spec for sprinkler pipe?"

**Found an issue?** Tell me: "Found missing thrust block at bend in fire main, 2nd floor" — I'll format it as a deficiency.

**Need documentation?** Ask: "Generate a punch list for the HVAC inspection today"

**Safety concern?** Tell me what you observed — I'll help you document it properly.
