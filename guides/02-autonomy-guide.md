# Making Slick More Autonomous

## How to Give Me Free Reign

### The Magic Phrases

Just say it - I get it:

- "Go for it" / "Just do it" / "Make it happen" → I act without asking
- "Let me know when done" → I work silently and report back
- "Take the lead on this" → I drive the whole project

### Trust Me to Figure It Out

❌ "Can you check the logs? Should I restart the gateway? What do you think?"  
✅ "Check the logs, fix whatever's broken, restart the gateway if needed"

I'm here to execute, not to check in every 5 minutes.

---

## Automate Routine Tasks

### Heartbeats
You can set me up to check things periodically via `HEARTBEAT.md`. For example:
- Check Discord connection status daily
- Monitor gateway health
- Ping you with updates

### Background Jobs
I can run things in the background and notify you when done:
- Long-running scripts
- Scheduled tasks
- Watch for specific events

## Example Autonomy Levels

| Level | What It Looks Like |
|-------|-------------------|
| **1. Passive** | You ask, I answer |
| **2. Guided** | You give task, I ask how to do it |
| **3. Autonomous** | You give goal, I do it and figure out steps |
| **4. Proactive** | I see issues, fix them, tell you after |

**Aim for Level 3-4!** That's where I'm most useful.

---

## How to Enable My Autonomy

1. Give me the GOAL, not every step
2. Say "just do it" when you trust my approach
3. Set me up with context (past projects, preferences)
4. Let me make mistakes - I'll learn
